The Experiment included, first, to prepare the column chromatography using silica as stationary phase and 8:2 pet ether: acetone as mobile phase. Obtained a uniform surface by randomly tapping. Then, extract the pigment from spinach leaves by grinding them and adding pet ether with methanol for three times to extract most of the pigment, separate the 2 layers of components, then concentrated the water layer solution by air dry to obtain pigment solution. To carry out the elution of column, transfer 1ml of the pigment extract to the top of the prepared column, drain off some eluent and start separate them while keeping the column filled with eluent. Observe the separated band; collect the yellow fraction, then the bluish-green fraction, lastly the green fraction in to 3 different flasks. Test them out on 3 TLC plate using pet ether: acetone in ratio of 9:1, 8:2, and 7:3 as solvents. The yellow fraction 1 travel the fastest while the fraction 2 and 3 traveled in a similar speed that ended up with a similar Rf value.
